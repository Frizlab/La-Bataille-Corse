When to bang ?

Good question !
There are many reasons for banging, if you want to get more cards.

The official rule (not always respected…) says you ought to bang only when two identical cards are played in a row.
This is known as the "Double" rule.

Example:

 

To make the game more exciting, other rules have been set up, allowing people to bang more often on the heap of cards or on the others' hands.
One or more extensions can be used, according to the age or skill of the players:

  • The Ten: you have to bang when the sum of the two last cards played amounts to ten (the ace evaluates to 1, court cards don't evaluate to anything).
Example:
  • The Sandwich: similar to the Double, but with whatever card between the two similar ones.
Example:
  • The Ten-Sandwich: a mix between the Sandwich and the Ten.
    That is, a Ten, but with any card in between.
Example:
  • The Begin-End is a Double involving the first and the last cards of the heap, instead of two cards in a row.
Example:
  • The Begin-End-Ten, when the sum of the first and the last card is ten.

There are also Hamburgers, also called Big-Macs, which are Sandwiches with two cards in between, and of course Hamburgers-Ten, aso.
